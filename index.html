What We Do

Research Areas

Our group investigates fundamental and applied aspects of photocatalysis, from nanostructured material synthesis to scalable environmental remediation systems.

⚗️

Photocatalysis & Solar Energy Conversion

Design of visible-light-driven heterogeneous photocatalysts via structural modification and composite engineering for hydrogen evolution and selective oxidation.

💧

Wastewater Remediation

Advanced oxidation processes and photocatalytic systems for the treatment of industrial effluents, dye removal, and emerging micropollutants in water bodies.

♻️

Circular Economy & Waste Valorization

Converting industrial by-products and natural waste streams into functional photocatalytic materials — closing the loop between pollution and resource recovery.

🌫️

CO₂ Capture & Utilization

Developing catalytic pathways for carbon dioxide valorization into value-added chemicals and fuels as part of a broader net-zero engineering strategy.

🖥️

Electronic Waste Recovery

Green hydrometallurgical and photochemical processes for selective extraction of precious metals from end-of-life batteries and printed circuit boards.

🌿

Biomass Conversion

Photo(electro)catalytic conversion of lignocellulosic biomass into platform chemicals, contributing to sustainable bio-based feedstock production.

About the Lab

The Chitiphon Research Group (CCC Lab) is based in the Department of Environmental and Sustainable Engineering at Chulalongkorn University, Bangkok. Our mission is to engineer innovative photocatalytic solutions that bridge fundamental material science with real-world environmental challenges.

We take a systems-level view — from synthesizing nanostructured catalysts to scaling processes that can be deployed in industrial settings. A core philosophy of our group is the valorization of waste: we routinely transform discarded materials into high-performance functional catalysts, demonstrating that environmental pollution and resource scarcity can be addressed simultaneously.

Our work spans energy conversion, water treatment, carbon management, and critical material recovery, with active collaborations across academic institutions and industry partners in the Asia-Pacific region.
