What we do

Research Areas

⚗️

Photocatalysis & Solar Energy

Design of visible-light-driven heterogeneous photocatalysts via structural modification and composite engineering for hydrogen evolution and pollutant degradation.

💧

Wastewater Remediation

Advanced oxidation processes and photocatalytic systems for the treatment of industrial effluents, dye removal, and emerging contaminants in water bodies.

♻️

Circular Economy & Upcycling

Converting industrial by-products and natural waste streams into functional photocatalytic materials — closing the loop between pollution and resource recovery.

🔋

CO₂ Capture & Utilization

Developing catalytic pathways for carbon dioxide valorization into value-added chemicals and fuels as part of a broader net-zero engineering strategy.

🖥️

Electronic Waste Recovery

Green hydrometallurgical and photochemical processes for selective extraction of precious metals from end-of-life batteries and printed circuit boards.

🌱

Biomass Conversion

Photo(electro)catalytic conversion of lignocellulosic biomass into platform chemicals, contributing to sustainable bio-based feedstock production.

About the Lab

The Chitiphon Research Group is based in the Department of Environmental and Sustainable Engineering at Chulalongkorn University, Bangkok. Our mission is to engineer innovative photocatalytic solutions that bridge fundamental material science with real-world environmental challenges.

We take a systems-level view — from synthesizing nanostructured catalysts to scaling processes that can be deployed in industrial settings. A key philosophy of our group is the valorization of waste: we routinely transform discarded materials into high-performance functional catalysts.

Our work spans energy conversion, water treatment, carbon management, and critical material recovery, collaborating with both academic institutions and industry partners across the Asia-Pacific region.
